Everything you need to know about warranty on water damages

It is a common disappointment among smartphone and laptop owners to discover that their standard manufacturer warranties (like AppleCare or manufacturer hardware guarantees) do not cover liquid damage. Even if you have a device rated with IP67 or IP68 water resistance, a splash of tea or an accidental drop in the pool instantly voids your coverage. Manufacturers rely on built-in **Liquid Contact Indicators (LCIs)**—tiny stickers that turn crimson when exposed to moisture—to immediately reject warranty claims.

Why does this happen? Because liquid ingress triggers a highly unpredictable process called **electrolytic corrosion**. When current-carrying traces on a logic board are bridged by water (especially containing minerals or acids), it leads to immediate short circuits. Over the following days, copper traces corrode and form green oxidation (copper carbonate), permanently destroying micro-components.

What should you do immediately?

  1. Power down instantly: Do not try to check if it works. Current running through a wet circuit is what kills it.
  2. Skip the rice: Rice dust gets inside ports and turns into a sticky paste, locking in moisture rather than drawing it out.
  3. Bring it to a lab: Independent premium labs like Robuzta Techlabs use specialized diagnostic equipment, ultrasonic cleaning baths (to lift minerals and oxidation), and microscopic micro-soldering to swap out individual blown resistors.

Unlike official service centers that force you to replace the entire logic board (costing up to 80% of the device's value), our technicians restore the existing board and offer a dedicated repair warranty on liquid restoration services.

Battery Science July 25, 2026

How actually wireless charging effects your battery life?

Wireless charging is undeniably convenient. Setting your phone down on a Qi-certified pad or snapping a MagSafe disc to the back eliminates cable clutter. However, the physical process behind wireless power transmission has a hidden side-effect on the lifespan of your lithium-ion battery.

Wireless charging relies on **magnetic induction**. An alternating current passes through a transmitter coil in the charging pad, creating an electromagnetic field. A receiver coil inside your device captures this field and converts it back into electrical current to charge the battery. This transfer is significantly less efficient than a copper cable; a portion of the energy is lost and dissipated as **heat**.

Heat is the absolute enemy of Lithium-Ion batteries. Batteries degrade naturally due to chemical aging, but exposure to temperatures above 35°C (95°F) accelerates this degradation dramatically. When a device sits on a wireless pad, the inductive coils generate constant localized heat directly underneath the battery. Over months of daily use, this thermal stress breaks down the battery’s internal chemistry, reducing its maximum charging capacity faster than standard wired charging.

Tips for healthier battery charging:

  • Remove thick cases: Rugged or thick plastic cases trap heat, preventing the device from cooling down during inductive charging.
  • Avoid overnight wireless charging: Keeping the device resting on a warm charger at 100% state-of-charge for hours maximums electrical and thermal stress.
  • Use certified accessories: Always choose Qi or MagSafe certified pads that include smart thermal management sensors to stop charging when temperatures rise.

Wired charging remains the cooler and faster option. If you do use wireless charging, do so in a cool environment and avoid using the phone simultaneously, as active screens create even more internal heat.
